Learn, Unlearn and Relearn

The twenty-first century is an era for ideation, development and certainly, the innovation. The rotation that the world is facing in technology, education and economics are at its apex. While the essential skills for this successful century include learning skills, literacy skills and life skills. Yet the statement of Alvin Toffler, a futurist made many educators to reinvigorate on the skills constructed in the dynamic modern world.

“The illiterate of the twenty-first century will not be those who cannot read and write, but those who cannot learn, unlearn and relearn”

Learn, Unlearn and Relearn’ is a success mantra in finding one’s niche. Let’s absorb the dictionary meaning of individual terms before delving into its contents. ‘Learn’ is to gain or acquire knowledge or skill in (something) by study, experience or being taught. ‘Unlearn’ is to discard (something learned especially false or outdated information) from one’s memory and `Relearn’ is to learn (something) again.

Learning takes place through formal or informal education. Formal education deals with schools and colleges and informal handles when someone teaches in private mode. Through this one is accustomed to `acquisitive learning’. This assists us to develop.

Unlearning is letting go of the established ways of acting and thinking. One may have the misconception of unlearning as wrong as it is oversimplified. It doesn’t remove or underestimate one’s knowledge. Unlearning can be challenging initially as many of us are used to acquisition. It elevates one’s knowledge up to-the-minute. For example, if one learns and gains some knowledge in a specific field, he or she can exhibit the knowledge to the requirement of the work station. To embrace the new work, he/she should know to unlearn the needless information and relearn the new conception in the field.

Relearning is the upskilling or reskilling to the demand of the job.

In the fast and dynamic world, change remains unchanged. He, who meets out his knowledge and a willingness to widen his perspective and embrace changes will survive and grow. As Darwin rightly puts forth, “In the struggle for survival, the fittest win out at the expense of their rivals because they succeed in adapting themselves best to their environment.”

As many jobs that existed a couple of decades ago have disappeared, more jobs are likely to disappear in coming years. Meanwhile new jobs requiring different skills and knowledge are emerging. Learning new skills equip individuals to perform different jobs as old ones are disposed or transformed.

Welcoming `Learn, Unlearn and Relearn’ helps the individual to ensure job stability and success.

Discover Yourself

Lunar expedition is no more a story; Mission to Mars is already on; planets of our solar system is in hands reach. Deep oceans can hold no mystery before our marine technology. Drones make the densest of forests a familiar terrain. Darkest caverns and bottomless abyss are explored now as never before. But the one unfathomable region humans dare not to explore is human himself. Human knowledge explored things far and wide but self- knowledge is untouched for. One can talk for hours about friends to fellows, inspired to ignored, but not more than few minutes about one’s own self. Scholars of ancient to modern time persist on one thing that can facilitate life’s journey- Knowledge of one’s true potentials and in this conjecture the age old but popular maxim “Know Thyself” is most pertinent. People who left an indelible mark on this planet, are the persons who explored their potentials above all their venture. Self-knowledge is the best of all knowledge for to put it in the words of Ralph Waldo Emerson “the World is the other me”.

We are at the cusp of time which forces to prove ourselves superior to machines. Everyone and everything is born with a unique potential but nature let us explore it. For some it takes one forth to half of their life time to identify their potential.

The ones who understand the innate talent of their own is an early bird who catches its worm soon. Having understood one’s own character and potential it’s easy to choose profession that matches the nature of an individual.

Life gives tips and clues to discover the hidden potentials that lies in every conscious and unconscious choice we make in daily basis. These questions can be put to one’s own self to explore the unchartered potentials within. Am I Compassionate? When did I cry last? When was the happiest of my lifetime? What do I like to do most? What is the task where I involve my whole self? Why I do this self-analysis? Where I want to be in near future? What values I uphold?

Answers to these questions may come quick or quiet late but they come unfailingly. Once got the answers, your life won’t be the same again.

Tips for Good CV/Resume

A good CV/Resume should be clear, concise and to the point without waffling.CV mostly used for Academic institutions and Resume for Industry positions CV will be lengthier compare to Resume as more detailed descriptions of coursework, research, publications or presentations

Tips of Resume

  • Choose a resume format & consistence usage of fonts
  • Bullet points wherever necessary instead of paragraph details
  • Within each section, list experiences in reverse chronological order, starting with the most recent
  • Qualify and Quantify wherever necessary
  • Proof read and stick to truth
  • Do not make general claims

Contents in resume

  • Header with Contact info
  • Professional Summary
  • Relevant skills for the job applied
  • Achievements/Certifications
  • Work Experience (with period)
  • Education details
  • co-curricular & Extra Curricular activities if worthy
  • Personal info
  • References: Optional (Do not have it in separate page/sheet)

Vocabulary

“Vocabulary is the matter of word- building as well as word using”. Vocabulary is very important for good communication. We cannot express our thoughts and feelings without vocabulary. A great number of words can help you to express your ideas in a meaningful manner. Vocabulary is the essential for learning the English language because, without adequate Vocabulary, we cannot express their ideas or understand others.

Some important ways to improve vocabulary

1. Read, read, daily

Reading improves one’s vocabulary. Cultivate the habit of reading books, newspaper, dictionary and blogs daily in order to improve one’s vocabulary. One should practice reading minimum of 15 minutes per day, it will develop a greater familiarity with language.

2. A word A Day

Visualize a new word from internet, dictionary or story books. Look for the meaning of the word and how to use the word in our daily life. This will help to improve our vocabulary.

3 Play Word Games

Word games offer another enjoyable way to up your language game. Sudoku and crossword puzzles are readily available from a variety of sources.

4. Running commentary

Running commentary helps us to search new vocabulary , as well as it will develop our language.

5. Set a goal

At the end of each week, create a quiz that includes the vocabulary. Taking a quiz or test can increase your ability to retain new words. Every time you learn a new root or word, create a flashcard.

6. Write

Besides reading, writing is the fastest way to develop an intimate knowledge of words. Writing forces us to think about the words we’re using in different ways to communicate an idea or develop a thought.

7. Practice using new words in Listening.

Listening to conversation, dialogues, and stories improves our vocabulary. It will help us to think for correct spellings and meaning of the word.

8. Start a blog

Starting a blog has never been easier, and having a reason to write something every day by having a blog will inevitably result in growth in your vocabulary over time.

9. Translate words into your own language

When you try to translate vocabulary words into your own language, they are committed to your mind much faster. You will learn to use them better as well.

10 Use pictures

Pictures can greatly enhance how fast we learn and memorize new words. So, try to learn vocabulary words and images associated with them.

11 Use flashcards.

A quick way to build a large vocabulary is to study a number of words via flashcards.Aiming for one new word a day is reasonable. You can always go for more, but it may not be reasonable to assimilate dozens of English words every single day.

10 things you should know when choosing to do an MBA

An MBA degree after Under Graduation is a conscious decision most people will make after concerted deliberation and meticulous planning. It is one of the prestigious professional courses that help students to understand the 360-degree dynamics in Business Management.

Here are my top 10 tips for getting the most out of your MBA program.

Get the best ROI - MBAs are expensive when compared to other professional courses. Aspirants pay hefty fees to complete the two-year program. However, there are only a handful of colleges that will ensure top-notch teaching and a good placement at the end of the course. Thus, Students must ensure to go through past placement records and whether the choice of college is worth every single penny.

Check Professors Expertise and Teaching Modules - Before joining your B-school/College, it is necessary to go through the professor's expertise in corporate and academics. It is necessary to check the teaching modules and evaluation techniques like caselet, quiz, industry visit, summer internship, etc.

Cultural Programs, Inter-college meetups, Research, Sports, etc - Before joining an institution students should check the various academic/Non-cultural programs, business cultural meetups, and the various research works carried in and outside college premises. It is important to check whether sports are emphasized for overall student development.

Collaborative and Teambuild with your classmates - Taking notes all by yourself is a tedious and strenuous process. It is better to surround yourself with colleagues who gives a helping hand in all assignment. Practice strong collaborative techniques and always be a team player.

Build a strong professional network with people inside and outside the college - It is very important to build a strong professional and personal network with your batch mates as well as your seniors. Ensure active social media participation in LinkedIn, Meetup, etc.

Build business attitude and master the art of reading - One of the most important aspects of business studies is to think out of the box and be practical while solving the business situation. Practice the art of reading current affairs, business journals like Harvard, Tech crunch, Bloomberg, Businessweek, Wired, etc for the latest business updates.

Giving back to community - Although Business studies are all about wealth and profit maximization, one should participate in Fundraisers and Charity Events and elevate their community. Donate Your Time to make a world of difference in the lives of many.

Be Proactive than Reactive - Sit in the front row, be engaged, ask questions. Don't be afraid of asking the wrong question, everybody does sooner or later. Try to be more proactive in doing assignments, industry visits, and even in non-academic areas. Initiate professional engagement with guest speakers in seminars and approach your faculties freely.

Make time to rest - Studying and complete coursework, assignments will likely take up much of your time when you pursue an MBA. . You may find that activities like meditation or yoga are just as helpful during stressful times.

Balance your work and social life - MBA consumes a lot of time in studies and academic activity. It is advised for students to strike a perfect work-life balance. If there is no fun, then there is no purpose in doing an MBA.

Department of Fashion Technology(SIGC)- "A Learning Hub to Budding Fashion Designers "

To make a career in fashion designing, one needs to have an affinity for dressing stylishly, a sense of originality, and creativity. Over the years, Fashion Industry across the globe has undergone tremendous changes, making India no exception to this global phenomenon. On another side, It is quoted that India still lags in producing successful and renowned fashion designers to compete in the global market.

Impact of Technology on Fashion Designing Industry

For Good Fashion Designer, Students are expected to learn new technologies as the profession involved converting ordinary objects into incredible and attractive art.

Below are few technological developments in the Fashion Industry -body scanning, Apparel Magic, 3D printing, 3D modeling, smart tailoring, need-based clothing.

Why choose Department of Fashion Technology (SIGC)

We at SIGC Trichy are one step further in bringing students closer to achieve their dream of becoming good fashion designers. We emphasize both practical and theory sessions to meet the industry standards. The examination and assessments are done on regular basis in a semester pattern. The students are evaluated both in terms of vocational, practical, and theoretical knowledge.

For the practical session, We have laboratories with the necessary tools to facilitate sewing, testing, and dyeing. We teach students computer-aided designing, Corel draw and Adobe Photoshop Software making students industry-ready before course completion.

References and Faculty Support

Students can access our Library having the latest reference books, Fashion Magazines, International Journals covering technical and management subjects. We have well-qualified staff with rich industry expertise in designing and technology.

With high job placement and all-around learning, the Department of Fashion technology aims at providing a comprehensive learning experience to build future fashion designers in the community.
