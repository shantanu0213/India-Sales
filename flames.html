FLAME UNIVERSITY’S
LIBERAL
EDUCATION

Our educational philosophy is rooted in the concept of Liberal Education. We believe that the real world does not function in isolation, and neither should knowledge. By interconnecting social sciences, humanities, physical sciences, business, design, natural sciences, fine & performing arts, communications management and computational sciences, we equip our students for an ever-changing new world. At FLAME, our students interact with various disciplines and are encouraged to address pressing world challenges through different perspectives. Our aim is to help them grow as critical thinkers and lifelong learners, as they lead purposeful lives, rather than just narrow professions. Therefore, we foster an environment that is personalised, participatory, and provides a transformational experience to aid in their personal and professional journeys.

YOUR JOURNEY WITH FLAME | RE-INVENTING LIBERAL EDUCATION. AGAIN.

We challenge what is known and believed about higher education. From re-inventing liberal education in India, to helping you chart your own unique career path; FLAME is not where you are, it is for who you are, and where you want to be.

Chart Unique Career Trajectories

Our multidisciplinary curriculum encourages you to explore what drives you and makes you unique. You can discover new avenues of growth and scout over 350 career combinations to make a place for yourself in the real world.

Uncover Questions, Discover Answers

We not only focus on finding answers to glaring world challenges, but also on creating new knowledge. With seven fully functional research centres, you can explore several faculty-student research opportunities.

FLAME University recently hosted Epilogue, a two-day literary event that featured a variety of activities, including a panel discussion, workshops, and fun events organized by student clubs. The highlight of the event was a panel discussion with renowned authors like Major General Ian Cardozo, a decorated war hero and author of "Cartoos Saab," who shared his inspiring experiences from the 1971 Indo-Pak War. Mugdha Sinha, an IAS officer and author of "Postcard Poems," offered unique perspectives on blending creativity with her work in public administration. Shabnam Minwalla, an award-winning author and journalist known for "Zen," captured the audience with her storytelling. Probal Dasgupta, an Indian Army veteran and author of "Camouflaged: Forgotten Stories from Battlefields," brought his powerful military experiences to life. Sanjeev Chopra, a distinguished historian and festival director of Valley of Words, added depth and insight to the discussions. The event was organized in collaboration with Valley of Words, an initiative that brings together writers, artists, critics, performers, craftspeople, readers, and listeners, and the Indus International School. We are thrilled to announce that Harsh Anjaria (@haarshie), FLAME Alumnus, has been awarded the prestigious Luis Buñuel Memorial Award 2024 for his documentary film, ‘Ollie!’. ‘Ollie!’ is a compelling documentary that unveils the unexpected and vibrant skateboarding community in Leh, Ladakh. The film captures the spirit of young skaters who redefine the cultural image of Ladakh, traditionally known for football and ice hockey. Set against Ladakh's breathtaking landscape, the documentary showcases how skateboarding brings meaning and purpose to individuals' lives and represents a growing subculture in one of India's most iconic regions. This esteemed recognition celebrates Harsh's exceptional directing, storytelling, and unwavering dedication to the art of filmmaking. We extend our heartfelt congratulations to him for this remarkable achievement.

We are pleased to announce that FLAME Alumni Aadi Sukhtankar, Arun Ravi (@arun_ravi23), and Srinithya Potlapalli presented their research paper titled "Handling of Natural Resources for New Housing Development in Pune City," Annual International Conference in Construction, Real Estate, Infrastructure, and Project Management (ICCRIP2024) held at NICMAR University, Pune. This research examines the challenges of sustainable resource management at residential construction sites in Pune, India, amidst rapid urban development. It finds misalignment among stakeholders and prioritization of short-term outcomes over long-term benefits hinder effective policy implementation. However, technologies like the Internet of Things (IoT) and Artificial Intelligence (AI) show promise for improving resource monitoring and stakeholder awareness. The study underscores the importance of collaborative efforts to align with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) for sustainable cities and responsible consumption, suggesting that such approaches can pave the way for a more sustainable future for Pune and other urban centers in India. This research was conducted under the mentorship of Prof. @shamitshrivastav, Faculty of Design, FLAME University and Prof. Andrea Phillott, Faculty of Environmental Studies, FLAME University. We extend our congratulations to Aadi, Arun, and Srinithya for their exemplary achievement.
